# NYSC CDS — Community Development Service

## What is CDS?
CDS stands for Community Development Service. It is one of the four cardinal 
programmes of NYSC and is COMPULSORY for all corps members throughout the 
service year. CDS is your secondary assignment — separate from your PPA (Place 
of Primary Assignment). Consistent absence from CDS can prevent you from 
receiving your monthly allowance and discharge certificate.

## CDS Schedule
- Meets ONCE per week on a fixed day determined by your Local Government Inspector (LGI)
- Runs from 8:00 AM to 12:00 PM (4 hours)
- You must have at least 50% attendance per month
- Missing over 50% means your PPA monthly clearance will NOT be signed
- If you miss due to illness, bring a medical certificate from a doctor
- General CDS (monthly meeting of all corps members in the LGA) is also compulsory

## The 16 Official CDS Groups
NYSC officially recognizes 16 CDS groups. Here they are with their functions:

1. EDUCATION GROUP — Teaching in local schools, organizing extra lessons, 
   adult literacy programmes. Best for: teachers, graduates in any field who 
   enjoy education.

2. HEALTH AND ENVIRONMENTAL SANITATION GROUP — Running free health campaigns, 
   medical outreaches, malaria and HIV/AIDS awareness. Best for: medical, 
   pharmacy, nursing, science graduates.

3. SAED GROUP — Supports and extends the SAED skill acquisition programme 
   into the community. Teaches vocational skills to locals. Best for: 
   corps members who took SAED seriously in camp.

4. AGRO-ALLIED AND FOOD SECURITY GROUP — Modern farming projects, 
   establishing farmers cooperatives, food production. Best for: 
   agriculture, biology, chemistry graduates.

5. ENVIRONMENTAL PROTECTION AND SANITATION GROUP (EPSG) — Tree planting, 
   drainage control, erosion prevention, community clean-up, landscaping. 
   Best for: geography, environmental science graduates. Note: involves 
   physical outdoor work.

6. LEGAL AID GROUP (CLAG — CORPS LEGAL AID GROUP) — Free legal services 
   to the less privileged, sensitization on human rights. Best for: 
   law graduates only.

7. ANTI-CORRUPTION GROUP (in collaboration with EFCC/ICPC) — Sensitizing 
   communities on the dangers of corruption, promoting integrity. 
   Best for: law, social sciences, mass communication graduates.

8. ROAD SAFETY GROUP (in collaboration with FRSC) — Traffic control 
   awareness, first aid to accident victims, road safety campaigns. 
   Best for: any graduate interested in public safety.

9. HIV/AIDS AWARENESS GROUP — Mobilizing and sensitizing communities on 
   HIV/AIDS prevention and treatment. Best for: health sciences, 
   social work graduates.

10. SOCIAL WELFARE AND DEVELOPMENT GROUP — Charity outreaches, improving 
    standard of living in host community. Best for: social work, 
    sociology, psychology graduates.

11. PRESS AND PUBLICATION GROUP — Information dissemination, 
    community journalism, creating newsletters and bulletins. 
    Best for: mass communication, English, journalism graduates.

12. SPORTS AND RECREATION GROUP — Organizing sports competitions, 
    promoting healthy lifestyles in the community. Best for: 
    sports science, physical education graduates or anyone who loves sports.

13. DRUG ABUSE AWARENESS GROUP (in collaboration with NDLEA/NAFDAC) — 
    Sensitization on dangers of drug abuse, eradicating fake goods. 
    Best for: pharmacy, health sciences, mass communication graduates.

14. ENTREPRENEURSHIP DEVELOPMENT GROUP — Business skills training 
    for community members, linking locals to funding. 
    Best for: business, economics, finance graduates.

15. DISASTER MANAGEMENT GROUP — Training communities on how to 
    manage floods, fire, and other disasters. 
    Best for: engineering, sciences graduates.

16. TECHNOLOGY AND ICT GROUP — Teaching computer skills, 
    digital literacy to community members. 
    Best for: computer science, IT, engineering graduates.

## Best CDS Groups to Join (by career benefit)
- If you work in tech: ICT Group — builds your teaching portfolio and community impact
- If you want networking: Entrepreneurship Group — meets business owners and funders  
- If you are in health/sciences: Health Group — builds clinical and public health experience
- If you want easy attendance: Sports Group — relaxed, enjoyable, good for networking
- If you are a lawyer: Legal Aid Group — real practical experience, professional exposure
- If you want recognition: Education Group — NYSC officials notice and appreciate teachers
- If you want to stay dry and indoors: Press Group, Anti-Corruption Group
- Avoid if you hate outdoor work: Environmental/EPSG Group (lots of sweeping and planting)

## CDS Rules Every Corps Member Must Know
- Wear your NYSC uniform on CDS days — it is compulsory
- You cannot attend your PPA on your CDS day — CDS takes priority
- Bring your Green Card / ID for attendance signing
- Projects must be approved by the NYSC Secretariat through your LGI
- If you receive a query for missing CDS, respond with a written letter immediately
- CDS is part of what determines if you pass out and receive your discharge certificate

## After Deployment: How to Find Your CDS Group
1. Report to your Local Government Inspector (LGI) after camp
2. Your LGI will assign you to a group based on your field of study
3. You can request a change if the assigned group does not match your skills
4. Attend your first CDS meeting to register your name on the attendance sheet
