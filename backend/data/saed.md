
# NYSC SAED — Skill Acquisition and Entrepreneurship Development

## What is SAED?
SAED stands for Skill Acquisition and Entrepreneurship Development. It was 
introduced by the Federal Government of Nigeria in 2012 to tackle unemployment 
by teaching corps members practical, income-generating skills. SAED is COMPULSORY 
during orientation camp. Poor participation affects your discharge certificate.
The official SAED platform is SAEDConnect at https://saedconnect.org

## How SAED Works in Camp
1. You attend SAED sensitization lectures in your first few days on camp
2. You choose ONE skill from the available categories that matches your interest
3. You attend introductory hands-on training sessions for that skill during camp
4. After camp, you can register for advanced post-camp training at accredited centres
5. Post-camp training is optional but strongly recommended

## Best SAED Skills to Join in 2025 (ranked by earning potential)
1. Digital Marketing — very low startup cost, remote work possible, high demand
2. Web Design and Development — one project can pay more than 3 months allowance
3. Solar Panel Installation — massive demand, technical skill, pays N50,000–N200,000 per job
4. Photography/Videography — Nollywood and event industry booming
5. Fashion Design — stable income, especially bridal market
6. Food Processing — low startup cost, can sell immediately
7. Graphics Design — freelance globally on Fiverr and Upwork

## How to Register for SAED
- In camp: attend the SAED sensitization lecture, then sign up on the form provided
- No prior experience needed — most skills start from the basics
- SAED training in camp is FREE
- Post-camp advanced training may require a small fee at accredited centres
- Register on SAEDConnect at https://saedconnect.org for online training and mentorship

## SAED Benefits
- Free training from professional trainers and experienced entrepreneurs
- Access to business loans and grants through NYSC partnerships
- Mentorship and networking with industry professionals
- eSAED Certificate upon completion at post-camp level
- NYSC may retain skilled corps members as trainers (earn extra income)
- SAEDConnect platform connects you to jobs, business partners, and funding

## Tips
- Choose a skill you can continue after service year, not just for camp
- If you already have a skill, inform the SAED coordinator — you may become a trainer
- Focus on ONE skill and master it rather than spreading yourself thin
- The best time to register is on your first or second day in camp
