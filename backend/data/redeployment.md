# NYSC Redeployment (Relocation) — Complete Guide 

## What is NYSC Redeployment? 
Redeployment (also called relocation) is the process of transferring from the state where you were originally posted to a different state. It is not automatic — you must apply and your request is only approved if it meets NYSC's established conditions. 

## Valid Reasons for Redeployment 
NYSC officially accepts only these four reasons: 

1. HEALTH GROUNDS — You have a chronic or severe medical condition (e.g. asthma, diabetes, epilepsy, heart disease) that cannot be adequately managed in your state of deployment. Requires a comprehensive medical report from a government or military hospital on the hospital's letterhead, including your diagnosis, doctor's name, stamp and signature. 

2. MARITAL GROUNDS — This is exclusively for married FEMALE corps members who wish to serve in the state where their husband resides. You must prove your husband actually lives in the state you are requesting. You cannot redeploy to Lagos, Abuja, your state of origin, or the state where you studied under marital grounds. 

3. SECURITY/INSECURITY GROUNDS — If you are posted to a state with recognized security challenges. NYSC only recognizes Borno, Yobe, and certain local governments in Adamawa State as insecure states. Being posted to any of these gives you near-automatic in-camp relocation. You cannot claim insecurity for other states. 

4. DG DIRECTIVE — The NYSC Director-General issues a special instruction for your redeployment. This requires connections and is unofficial. 

## Required Documents 

For Health grounds: 
- Comprehensive medical report from a government or state hospital 
- Must be on hospital letterhead with diagnosis, doctor's name, stamp and signature 
- Recent test results or medical imaging (e.g. X-rays) if applicable 

For Marriage grounds: 
- Marriage certificate 
- Affidavit of marriage 
- Newspaper publication of name change 
- Valid ID of spouse (national ID, passport, or voter's card) 
- Proof of spouse's domicile in requested state (utility bills, employer letter) 
- Each uploaded file must not exceed 200KB 

## How to Apply — In Camp (Recommended) 
1. During the second week of your 21-day orientation course, a Relocation Slip is distributed to interested corps members 
2. Fill the slip with your reason and supporting documents 
3. Submit to camp officials 
4. Results are usually announced on the last day of camp 

## How to Apply — Online (After Camp) 
1. Log in to your NYSC portal dashboard at `https://portal.nysc.org.ng` 
2. Navigate to the "Relocation/Redeployment" section 
3. Select your reason for relocation 
4. Choose the state you want to be redeployed to 
5. Upload supporting documents (each file must not exceed 200KB) 
6. Click Submit — if you see a success message, your application has been received 
7. Wait for review — approval typically takes a few weeks to a few months 

## After Approval 
 - Log in to the portal and download your redeployment letter 
 - Visit the NYSC office in your new state and ensure your records are updated 
 - Inform your new Local Government Inspector (LGI) and provide necessary documents 
 - You can re-apply after 3 months if your first application is denied 

## Important Rules 
 - Redeployment is FREE — NYSC does not charge for it. Beware of scammers 
 - You cannot redeploy to your state of origin, the state where you studied, or Lagos and Abuja under health/marriage grounds 
 - Apply as early as possible — in-camp applications are processed faster 
 - Continue serving in your original state unless your redeployment is approved 
 - Male corps members cannot apply for marital redeployment 
 - Medical professionals cannot use health grounds to redeploy to Lagos or Abuja
