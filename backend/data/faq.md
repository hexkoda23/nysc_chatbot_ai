# NYSC Frequently Asked Questions

## Q: What documents do I need to register for NYSC?
A: For online registration you need your NIN, JAMB registration number, matriculation number, passport photograph (white background), and scanned signature. Your name must be on your school's Senate List. For camp registration you additionally need your call-up letter (colour print, no lamination), Green Card (colour print, no lamination), statement of results (original + 4 copies), final year ID card (original + 2 copies), 8 passport photographs, and a Medical Certificate of Fitness from a government hospital.

## Q: How do I check if my name is on the Senate List?
A: Go to `https://portal.nysc.org.ng/nysc3/CheckInstitutionCoursesPCMs.aspx`  — select your batch, program type, institution, and course, then click Extract PCM. Alternatively go to `https://portal.nysc.org.ng/nysc2/VerifySenateLists.aspx,`  select your institution, enter your Matriculation Number and Surname, input your Date of Birth, and click Search. If a green tick appears, you are approved for mobilization. If "No Record Found" appears, contact your school's Student Affairs Office immediately — your school may not have uploaded the list yet or there may be an error.

## Q: My name is not on the Senate List, what should I do?
A: Visit your institution's Student Affairs Office immediately. Confirm your graduation details were submitted to NYSC. If there is an error (wrong spelling, wrong date of birth), report it to your school so they can correct and re-upload. Outstanding fees or unreturned library books can also block your inclusion — clear all pending obligations. Keep checking the portal regularly as updates happen in batches.
